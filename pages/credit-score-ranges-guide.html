FAQ

Common questions

Is 700 a good credit score?

In many lending contexts, yes. A 700 score is commonly considered good and can open more competitive rates than a fair-credit profile.

Why do lenders care about ranges instead of exact points?

Because underwriting systems usually group applicants into pricing tiers rather than crafting a unique rate for every single score point.

Can two people with the same score get different rates?

Yes. Income, down payment, debt-to-income ratio, cash reserves, and lender-specific policy all still matter.

Do mortgage lenders use the same scores as card issuers?

Not always. Mortgage underwriting often uses older FICO versions and more detailed documentation.

Should I wait to borrow until I move to the next range?

Sometimes. If a small improvement could unlock meaningfully better pricing and your need is not urgent, waiting can make financial sense.
